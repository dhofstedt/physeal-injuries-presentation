Stressed or Fractured?

MRI Differentiating Indicators of Physeal Injury

Introduction

Sports injuries in children are common. Distinguishing chronic stress from acute fractures is challenging.

Study Data

Analysis of 83 injuries revealed 79% ZPC discontinuity in chronic stress cases and 83% periosteal injury in acute fractures.

Clinical Impact

MRI findings guide management: chronic stress injuries benefit from rest and physical therapy, while acute fractures require immobilization.

Conclusion

MRI offers key indicators to differentiate chronic physeal stress from acute Salter-Harris fractures. Integrate imaging with clinical history for optimal care.

Future Directions

Long-term MRI follow-up may refine diagnostic criteria. Further research is needed to validate these imaging markers.
