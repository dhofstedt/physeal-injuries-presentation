Stressed or Fractured?

MRI Differentiating Indicators of Physeal Injury

Daniel Hofstedt, DO

February 11, 2025 | MSK Journal Club

Introduction

Epidemiological Context

30M+ U.S. children in organized sports

10% annual sport-related injury rate

Introduction

Clinical Significance

Physis = weakest link in immature skeleton

MRI critical for accurate diagnosis

Introduction

Study Objectives

Identify MRI markers distinguishing:

  • Chronic stress
  • Acute fractures
  • Acute-on-chronic injuries

Physeal Anatomy

Structural Organization

Three functional zones:

  1. Resting
  2. Proliferative
  3. Hypertrophic

Growth Mechanisms

Endochondral Ossification

Key structures:

  • Cartilage template
  • Zone of Provisional Calcification (ZPC)

Chronic Stress Injury

Key Features:

  • Diffuse physeal widening
  • Intact periosteum
  • ZPC irregularities

Acute Fracture

Hallmark Signs:

  • Discrete fracture line
  • Periosteal disruption
  • Marrow edema pattern

Acute-on-Chronic Injury

Composite Findings:

  • Baseline physeal widening
  • Superimposed fracture line
  • Mixed edema patterns

Key Differentiator 1

Zone of Provisional Calcification

Pathological Patterns:

  • Chronic: Irregular thinning
  • Acute: Focal disruption

Key Differentiator 2

Periosteal/Perichondrial Integrity

Critical Observations:

  • Chronic: Intact layers
  • Acute: Elevation/rupture

Side-by-Side Comparison

Chronic vs Acute Findings:

  • Edema distribution
  • Marrow changes
  • Soft tissue involvement

Study Data

Analysis of 83 injuries revealed 79% ZPC discontinuity in chronic stress cases and 83% periosteal injury in acute fractures.

Clinical Impact

MRI findings guide management: chronic stress injuries benefit from rest and physical therapy, while acute fractures require immobilization.

Conclusion

MRI offers key indicators to differentiate chronic physeal stress from acute Salter-Harris fractures. Integrate imaging with clinical history for optimal care.

Future Directions

Long-term MRI follow-up may refine diagnostic criteria. Further research is needed to validate these imaging markers.
